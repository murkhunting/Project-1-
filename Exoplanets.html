Exoplanetary Guide

What is an Exoplanet?

The official definition of the term "planet" used by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) only covers the Solar System and thus does not apply to exoplanets.

Objects that are outside the Solar System with true masses below the limiting mass for thermonuclear fusion of deuterium (currently calculated to be 13 Jupiter masses for objects of solar metallicity) that orbit stars or stellar remnants are considered "planets" or "Exoplanets".

Why the Exoplanets are so important?

The discovery of exoplanets has intensified interest in the search for extraterrestrial life. There is special interest in planets that orbit in a star's habitable zone, where it is possible for liquid water, a prerequisite for life on Earth, to exist on the surface.
